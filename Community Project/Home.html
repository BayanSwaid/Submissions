Intro

While seasonal bottlenecks are common in the world, Lebanon suffers from a chronic traffic problem that dates back to the 1990s, when the 15-Year Lebanese civil war finally ended. Since then, the situation has aggravated due to the deteriorating condition of the road network, the absence of a reliable public transportation system, and recently the influx of more than one million officially registered Syrian refugees escaping the war in their home country.
